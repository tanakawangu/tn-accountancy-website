Individuals

You may need to complete a Self Assessment tax return if you have income that is not fully taxed at source. This can include rental income, savings interest, investment income, dividends, freelance income, or other earnings outside of employment.

Many individuals also find themselves required to file a return for the first time and are unsure where to begin. We can help you understand what information is needed, prepare your return, and meet the filing deadlines.

Sole Traders

Running a business as a sole trader means you are personally responsible for keeping accurate records and reporting your income and expenses through Self Assessment each year. Getting this right is important — not just for tax purposes, but to understand how your business is performing.

We support sole traders with bookkeeping, accounts preparation, Self Assessment returns, and VAT where applicable. Whether you are newly self-employed or have been trading for years, we can help you stay organised.

Landlords

Rental income must be declared to HMRC through Self Assessment. As a landlord, you are required to report your rental income, and you may be able to deduct certain allowable expenses from that income. Keeping clear records of your rental income, mortgage interest (subject to current rules), letting agent fees, repairs, and other costs is important.

Many landlords find that their records are not as organised as they need to be when the Self Assessment deadline approaches. We can help you get your rental records in order and prepare your return accurately.

Contractors

Contractors working through limited companies or as sole traders need to maintain accurate records, manage their invoicing, and meet regular filing obligations. Directors of contractor companies also need to complete a personal Self Assessment return each year.

We provide bookkeeping, accounts, Self Assessment, VAT, and Corporation Tax support for contractors, helping you keep your finances organised and your filing up to date.

Limited Companies and Directors

Limited companies have specific statutory obligations with both HMRC and Companies House — including annual accounts, Corporation Tax returns, Confirmation Statements, and (where applicable) VAT returns and payroll. Directors are also usually required to complete a personal Self Assessment tax return.

We support limited companies with the full range of accountancy needs including bookkeeping, annual accounts, Corporation Tax, VAT, payroll, and director's Self Assessment.

Directors

Directors of limited companies have both personal and corporate financial responsibilities. On the personal side, most directors must submit a Self Assessment tax return each year. On the corporate side, the company must file annual accounts, Corporation Tax returns, and meet its Companies House obligations.

Staying on top of both sets of responsibilities requires organised records and a clear understanding of deadlines. We support directors with both their personal and company accounting needs.
